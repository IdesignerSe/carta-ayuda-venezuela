Humanitarian Support Request

An open letter appealing to rescue organizations worldwide to assist in the life‑saving search and rescue efforts underway in Venezuela.
We are aware that the United Nations and the Venezuelan Red Cross are coordinating efforts.

Dear Sir or Madam,

I am writing with deep respect and concern regarding the current situation in Venezuela, following the recent earthquakes that have caused an undetermined number of missing persons, thousands of injured individuals, and severe structural damage across multiple states.

As a Venezuelan currently living abroad, I feel a strong responsibility to collaborate in a responsible and coordinated manner with specialized search and rescue organizations, as well as humanitarian teams that are working or may be able to work in the affected areas.

The magnitude of this disaster far exceeds the operational capacity of local authorities. According to international standards, an event involving dozens of collapsed buildings, more than 250 severely damaged structures, and thousands of potentially trapped individuals requires:

➡️ Between 800 and 1,500 specialized rescuers working simultaneously

This includes:

  • Heavy USAR teams
  • Medium USAR teams
  • K9 search units
  • Structural firefighters
  • Emergency medical teams

Unfortunately, Venezuela does not have the resources or personnel required to meet this demand within the critical survival window. Every hour lost drastically reduces the chances of rescuing people alive.

For this reason, this letter serves as an open request for your organization to consider self‑deploying through the humanitarian channels already activated by the United Nations, the Red Cross, and USAR coordination teams.

As a citizen, my intention is to support official efforts while respecting all operational protocols and humanitarian priorities. I am willing to assist with information sharing, remote coordination, and any logistical support that may facilitate your participation.

I deeply appreciate the work you do and the commitment you represent to the world in moments like this.

Sincerely,
